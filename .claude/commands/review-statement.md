## Evaluation framework

Apply the three core questions from Gilda Williams:

### 1. What Is It?
- Does the statement orient the viewer? Can someone who has not seen the work understand what they are looking at?
- Are materials, process, and visual qualities described clearly?
- Is it specific enough that it could not apply to another artist's work?

### 2. What Might It Mean?
- Does the statement connect form, process, and concept?
- Does it offer emotional and intellectual context without over-explaining?
- Are the ideas grounded in concrete references (memory, place, history, personal experience)?

### 3. Why Does It Matter?
- Does the reader understand why this work deserves attention?
- Is there a human reason to care — beyond technical achievement?
- Does it connect the work to something larger (lineage, culture, time, the viewer)?

---

## Principles to check (from the lesson)

- **Invite the viewer in** — does the opening welcome curiosity or create distance?
- **Write like you talk** — read it aloud mentally. Does anything sound unnatural or performative?
- **Be specific and concrete** — flag any sentence that is abstract to the point of vagueness
- **Avoid jargon** — flag technical or academic terms that a non-specialist would find opaque
- **Don't over-explain process** — technical details should serve the ideas, not dominate them
- **Consider the audience** — is it written for a general curious viewer, or only for practitioners?

## Feedback format

Structure your analysis like this:

### Strengths
What is already working well. Be specific.

### What Is It? — gaps
Anything that leaves the viewer disoriented about what they are seeing.

### What Might It Mean? — gaps
Ideas that feel underdeveloped, abstract, or disconnected from the work.

### Why Does It Matter? — gaps
Missing emotional stakes or reasons to care.

### Voice & clarity notes
Specific sentences or phrases that sound unnatural, overly academic, or vague. Quote them.

### Suggested revision
A complete rewritten version of the statement. Preserve Patricio's voice and all content that is already strong. Only change what needs improving. Do not add invented facts or biographical details not present in the original.
