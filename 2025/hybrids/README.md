So much of my work, and actually, on the essence of who I am, is about of bridging and translating.
About navigating the between fields, disciplines and cultures. At the beginning you rely on mapping. 
Like, on traditional painting, finding relationships between landmarks.

* The face can be divide in three equal sections.
* The lower end of the ears align with the end of the nose. 
* The distance between the eyes is equal to the width of one eye. 
* The width of the mouth is equal to the distance between the pupils.
* etc.

Then, you start filling that analytical structure with your own experience. Your perception of the local color, the surface relations to planes, the plane direction relation with light, etc. And so, you slowly deviate from the map to a deep censorial experience of the territory. Then after that if you are lucky, becomes a more transcendent experience, communion with the sitter, a silent dialogue with their presence.  

In this series of paintings, I offload the mapping to the machine, so I can indulge myself on the experience of the territory. On the subjective exploration of the subject to paint. Like the camera obscura on the Renascence, the Flemish development of the first oil mediums techniques, the conquer of the outside lighting that paint tubes enable the impressionism, the conceptual exploration through photography for Dada and Fluxus, and so many more. All of these are examples of how the use of technology could free those artist willing to experiment with the new and untamed technology.

I build my first plotter for [my Skylines, my MFA Thesis Project at Parsons in 2024](/2014/skylines/). Since then I have been interested in the idea of the machine as a collaborator, and how to better create digital art tools. For this series of paintings I created a custom python script that uses computer vision to analyze an image, extracting landmarks, proportions and other features. Then, these features are translated to a canvas using a plotter.

Once the machine is done with the mapping, I take over the canvas with my brushes and oil colors, to explore and inhabit that space, free of the responsibility of being objective and precise, and compelled to be subjective and intuitive.
